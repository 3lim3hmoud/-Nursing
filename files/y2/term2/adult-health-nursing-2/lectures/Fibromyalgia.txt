Definition 
 A chronic disorder characterized by widespread 
musculoskeletal pain, often accompanied by 
fatigue, sleep disturbances, memory issues, and 
mood changes

• More common in women (80–90% of cases). 
• Often coexists with conditions like irritable bowel 
syndrome (IBS), migraine, chronic fatigue 
syndrome, and depression.

Tests to rule out other conditions: 
• • CBC,  ESR,  CRP 
• • Thyroid function tests (TSH) 
• •  RF  
• • Vitamin D levels

Management  
Pharmacological (Medications ) 
• Analgesics 
• Antidepressants 
• Anticonvulsants  
• Muscle relaxants  
• Sleep aids

Management  
Non phamacological / pain management  
• Physical therapy; exercise programs, Avoid overexertion – 
balance activity and rest 
• Stress management techniques; deep breathing exercises, 
meditation  
• Massage, warm bath, heat therapy

Management  
Promotion of sleep  
• Encourage regular bedtime routine 
• Limit caffeine and electronics before bed 
• Provide information on sleep hygiene

Management  
Emotional Support & health education  
• Provide reassurance and active listening 
• Refer to mental health services if needed 
• Encourage support group participation 
• Educate on chronic nature of disease and need for lifestyle 
changes 
• Reinforce importance of stress reduction 
• Discuss medication side effects and adherence
