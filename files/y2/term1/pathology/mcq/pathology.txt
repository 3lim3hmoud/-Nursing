Part III: Enumeration

1. Causes of Inflammation 
• Living irritants (bacteria, viruses, parasites, fungi) 
• Physical irritants (excess heat, cold, radiation) 
• Chemical irritants (acids, alkalis, poisons) 
• Mechanical irritants (trauma, friction) 
• Immunological (allergic inflammation) 
 
2. Factors Affecting Repair 
Local factors: 
• Wound type and size 
• Movement 
• Infection 
• Blood supply 
• Foreign material 
• Radiation 
General factors: 
• Age 
• General health (e.g., diabetes) 
• Drugs (e.g., steroids) 
• Cardiovascular status 
• Dietary deficiencies (protein, vitamin C) 
 
3. Complications of Repair 
• Insufficient fibrosis (ulcer, sinus, fistula) 
• Excessive fibrosis (keloid) 
• Excessive contraction (contractures, strictures)

• Epidermal cyst (implantation cyst) 
 
4. Types of Gangrene 
• Dry gangrene 
• Moist (wet) gangrene 
• Infective gangrene 
(gas gangrene, lung gangrene, bed sores, cancrum oris) 
 
5. Fates of Thrombus 
• Lysis 
• Organization 
• Recanalization 
• Embolization 
• Calcification 
• Occlusion 
• Infection 
 
6. Routes of Spread of Malignant Tumours 
• Direct spread (invasion) 
• Lymphatic spread 
• Vascular (blood) spread 
• Transcoelomic spread 
• Perineural spread 
 
7. Components of Granuloma 
• Epithelioid cells (modified macrophages)

• Giant cells (Langhans or foreign body type) 
• Lymphocytes 
• Fibroblasts 
 
Part V: Essential Enumerations 
Evidence of Malignant Transformation 
1. Increased rate of growth 
2. Loss of cellular differentiation 
3. Infiltration of capsule and surrounding tissue 
Fate of a Septic Thrombus 
• Fragmentation → septic emboli → pyemic abscesses 
Mechanism of Edema in Heart Failure 
• Increased capillary hydrostatic pressure 
• Salt and water retention (aldosterone) 
• Increased permeability due to hypoxia
