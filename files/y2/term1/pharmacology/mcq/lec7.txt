Part II: Essay Hints 
1. Definition: Sustained increase in BP (>130/80). Table: Normal (<120/80), Pre-HTN 
(120-139/80-89), Stage 1 (140-159/90-99), Stage 2 (≥160/100). 
2. Complications: Brain stroke, Heart failure, Myocardial Infarction, Renal failure, 
and Retinopathy. 
3. Primary: 80-90%, Idiopathic (salt, stress, smoking). Secondary: 10-20%, caused by 
endocrine disease, renal disease, or drugs. 
4. Baroreflex: Increase in BP → distension of aorta/carotid → stimulates 
baroreceptors → impulses to vasomotor center → decreased Sympathetic and 
increased Parasympathetic stimulation. 
5. $\alpha$-Methyl dopa: Converted to $\alpha$-methyl NE, displaces NE from 
vesicles (less $\alpha1$ effect) and stimulates $\alpha2$ receptors (decreased 
release). Used in pregnancy and renal-complicated hypertension. 
6. Uses: Angina/MI, Supraventricular arrhythmias, Migraine prophylaxis, Glaucoma, 
Hyperthyroidism, and Anxiety. 
7. Contraindications: Congestive heart failure (-ve inotropic), Bronchial asthma 
(non-selective), and Diabetes (especially Propranolol).
