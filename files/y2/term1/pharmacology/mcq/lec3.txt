Part II: Essay Hints (Summary of Source Info) 
1. Definition: "What the drug does to the body". Three areas: Mechanism of action, 
pharmacological actions, and adverse effects. 
2. Stimulation: Increases activity of specialized cells (e.g., Adrenaline). Depression: 
Decreases activity of specialized cells (e.g., Barbiturates). 
3. Physical Action:  
o Osmosis: 20% Mannitol for edema. 
o Adsorption: Activated charcoal for poisoning. 
o Demulcent: Cough syrup coating mucosa. 
o Radioactivity: 131I for hyperthyroidism. 
4. Chemical Action: Antacids (weak bases) neutralize stomach acid. Chelating agents 
trap metals in ring structures to form soluble complexes for excretion. 
5. Allopurinol: Inhibits the enzyme xanthine oxidase, which prevents the oxidation 
of xanthine/hypoxanthine into uric acid. 
6. Anesthetics vs SSRIs: Local anesthetics block sodium channels. SSRIs bind to 5-HT 
transporters to block serotonin reuptake.

7. Receptors: Macromolecules on cell surfaces, cytoplasm, or nucleus. They bind 
drugs to produce cellular changes, acting like a "Lock" that specific drug "Keys" fit 
into.
