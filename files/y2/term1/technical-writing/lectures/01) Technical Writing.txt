Course Aim:
A technical writing in nursing course is designed to equip nurses with 
➢ The precise, 
➢ Clear, and 
➢ Audience-specific communication skills 
  essential for their profession. 
Unlike general academic writing, this course focuses on 
➢ Producing objective, 
➢ Evidence-based documents 
  for legal, clinical, and research purposes..

3
Course Objectives:
Upon successful completion of the course, students should be able to:
➢ Apply principles of clarity, conciseness, and accuracy to all 
forms of nursing documentation.
➢ Select the appropriate tone and style for different audiences, 
from professional peers to laypersons.
➢ Master different methods of nursing documentation and 
reporting, such as source-oriented, problem-oriented, and 
charting-by-exception.
➢ Understand the legal and ethical implications of nursing 
documentation and reporting..

4
Course Objectives (Cont.):
Upon successful completion of the course, students should be able to:
➢ Navigate and document effectively within electronic health 
records (EHR) systems.
➢ Develop evidence-based patient education materials, including 
pamphlets, brochures, and instructional guides.
➢ Write formal documents, such as proposals, policies, 
procedures, and research reports, adhering to professional 
standards like APA format.
➢ Create and utilize graphics, such as charts and tables, to 
enhance reports and presentations..

Definition/Purpose of Course?
 Technical communication is the “art and science of making 
complex technical information accessible, usable, and relevant 
to a variety of audiences in a variety of settings.”
 Composition of the types of documents technical professionals 
write.
 Review of the planning, drafting, and editing processes.
 Interaction with new technologies and new environments..
