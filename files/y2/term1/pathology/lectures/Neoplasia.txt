Carcinoma in Situ
• It is sometimes named: Intraepithelial Neoplasia, Intraepithelial 
Carcinoma or Pre-invasive Carcinoma)
• Gross picture: difficult to be detected, some cases may cause a thick 
indurated patch.
• Microscopic picture:
1. Cytologic features of atypia (pleomorphism, Hyperchromasia, loss of 
polarity and abnormal mitosis)
2. NO invasion to basement membrane
• Fate: Progression into invasive carcinoma occurs after a variable time
• Examples:
• Duct carcinoma in situ in breast (DCIS)
• Prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PIN)
• Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)
