Definition:
It is an inflammatory disease of the
joints, originates in the synovium, a soft
tissue membrane that lines and protects
joints.
The joints become swollen, stiff and 
painful. This can happen in one or several 
joints particularly fingers, wrists, knees 
and ankles

Diagnostic assessment: -
❖ Presence of manifestations. 
❖ Rheumatoid Factor (RF) found in 80% of 
patients.
❖ C-reactive protein (CRP)
❖ ESR
❖ Decreased RBC and Cr

Management 
Nursing Interventions :
– Pain relief measures
– Relief of fatigue
– Increasing mobility
– Monitoring for potential complications
– Increase knowledge of disease
– Promoting self-care

Management 
Drug Therapy: its goal : 
• Relieve pain
• Reduce inflammation
• Stop / slow joint damage and deformity
• Improve well-being and ability to function

Medications
• NSAIDS
• Steroids (PO or intra-articular)

Arthrocentesis ( aspiration fluid from joint) and x-rays can 
be performed
Surgical management : Joint replacement
