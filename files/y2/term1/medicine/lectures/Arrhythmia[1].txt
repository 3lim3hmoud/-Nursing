LearningObjectives
By the end of this lecture, students will be able to:
Define what an arrhythmia is.
Describe normal cardiac conduction and rhythm.
Identify common types of arrhythmias.
Recognize signs, symptoms, and risk factors.
Discuss nursing assessment and management for patients 
with arrhythmias.

Definition
An arrhythmia is an abnormality in the 
rate, rhythm, or sequence of cardiac 
electrical impulses.
Normal heart rate: 60–100 beats per 
minute (bpm)
Normal rhythm: Sinus rhythm — impulse 
originates from the sinoatrial (SA) node

Normal Cardiac Conduction System 
Pathway:
SA node → AV node → Bundle of His → Right & 
Left Bundle Branches → Purkinje fibers
Key concept:
Electrical activity triggers mechanical contraction
of the heart. Disturbance in this system = 
arrhythmia.

Type Example Heart Rate Description
Bradyarrhythmias Sinus bradycardia, 
AV block <60 bpm Slow rhythm
Tachyarrhythmias Sinus tachycardia, 
SVT , VT >100 bpm Fast rhythm
Irregularrhythms Atrial fibrillation, 
PVCs Variable Irregular beat 
pattern

Common Arrhythmias and ECG Features
A. Sinus Bradycardia
•HR < 60 bpm, regular rhythm
•Often normal in athletes or during sleep
•Causes: Vagal stimulation, medications (beta-
blockers, digoxin)
•Treatment: If symptomatic → Atropine, oxygen, 
IV fluids

B. Sinus Tachycardia
•HR > 100 bpm, regular rhythm
•Causes: Fever, pain, anxiety, 
dehydration, heart failure
•Treatment:Treat the underlying cause

C. Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)
•Irregularly irregular rhythm, no P waves
•Risk: Stroke, thromboembolism
•Symptoms: Palpitations, fatigue, shortness of breath
•Management:
• Rate control: Beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers
• Anticoagulation: Warfarin, DOACs
• Cardioversion if indicated

D. Atrial Flutter
•“Sawtooth” pattern on ECG
•Often regular, 250–350 bpm atrial 
rate
•Management:Similar to AFib

E. Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)
•Wide QRS complexes, rate >100 bpm
•Life-threatening!
•Causes: Myocardial infarction, electrolyte imbalance
•Treatment:
• With pulse: Amiodarone, synchronized 
cardioversion
• Pulseless: CPR + Defibrillation

F . Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)
•Chaotic, no organized rhythm or 
cardiac output
•Emergency!
•Management:CPR, Defibrillation 
immediately

G. Asystole
•Flat line (no electrical activity)
•Management:CPR, Epinephrine, treat 
reversible causes (H’s & T’s)

5. 
 Nursing Assessment
•Monitor vital signs and ECG rhythm strips
•Assess for signs of decreased cardiac output:
• Hypotension
• Dizziness
• Chest pain
• Shortness of breath
• Altered mental status
•Check medications, electrolytes, and oxygen levels

6. 
 Nursing Management & Interventions
1.Maintain airway, breathing, circulation (ABCs)
2.Apply oxygenas ordered
3.Continuous ECG monitoring
4.Administer antiarrhythmic drugsas prescribed
5.Prepare for emergency procedures:
1.Defibrillation
2.Cardioversion
3.Temporary pacing

7. 
 Complications
Decreased cardiac output
Thromboembolism (especially in AFib)
Sudden cardiac arrest
Heart failure

8. 
 Summary
•Arrhythmias are disturbances in heart rhythm.
•ECG interpretation is key to diagnosis.
•Prompt recognition and intervention save lives.
•Nurses play a crucial role in monitoring, 
treatment, and patient education.
