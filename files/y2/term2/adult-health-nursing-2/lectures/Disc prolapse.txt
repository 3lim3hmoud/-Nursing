Definition 
 A disc prolapse occurs when the (nucleus 
pulposus); soft inner core of a spinal disc bulges 
out and press on surrounding nerves or spinal 
cord.

O It most commonly affects the lumbar 
spine (L4-L5, S1) but can also occur in 
the cervical or thoracic spine 
O Cervical  disc prolapse 
O Thoracic disc prolapse 
O lumber disc prolapse

• Degenerative disc disease ; age-related wear and tear 
• Trauma or injury ;e.g., lifting heavy objects improperly 
• Repetitive strain or twisting motions 
• Obesity ; increased spinal load 
• Poor posture 
• Sedentary lifestyle 
• Genetic predisposition

Management  
Non-Surgical 
•  Bed rest (short-term) – avoid prolonged immobility 
• NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen) 
• Muscle relaxants 
• Oral steroids or epidural steroid injections 
• Hot/cold compresses 
• Activity modification (avoid lifting, bending)

•  Physiotherapy: 
•Core strengthening 
•Stretching exercises 
•Postural training

Surgical 
•  Discectomy: removal of the herniated portion of the 
disc 
• Endoscopic discectomy 
• Laminectomy: removal of part of the vertebral bone

Nursing process   
1- Assessment : 
•Assess s&s ; complete assessment of pain 
•Observe for numbness, tingling, muscle weakness 
•Monitor gait and mobility 
•Check for signs of bowel/bladder dysfunction 
•Evaluate impact on ADLs (activities of daily living)

Nursing process   
2- Nursing diagnoses : 
• Acute Pain related to nerve compression  
• Impaired physical mobility related to pain and 
discomfort 
• Anxiety related to limited mobility 
• Deficient knowledge related to condition and self-
care 
• Risk for injury related to muscle weakness or 
sensory deficits

Nursing process   
3,4 - Patient goal & Nursing interventions :  
 goal : patient will report reduced pain / Pain Management 
• Apply hot/cold compresses 
• Encourage use of firm mattress or back support 
• Position patient with knees slightly bent (lumbar cases) 
• Administer analgesics, NSAIDs, muscle relaxants as 
prescribed

Nursing process   
goal : Patient will maintain mobility and function. 
Mobility support  
• Encourage mobilization as tolerated 
• Assist with physical therapy 
• Teach log-rolling technique when turning in bed 
• Use of braces or lumbar support belts if prescribed
