Definition:
is a break or interruption in
the continuity of a bone

❖ occur mostly from trauma but can result from a
pathologic process in which bone has degenerated, such
as in osteoporosis (metabolic bone disorder that causes a
decrease in bone mass).

Complete & incomplete fracture
Closed & open fracture 
Comminuted fracture
Types of Fractures
Greenstick fracture

• Complete fracture: bone breaks into two parts that 
are completely separated.
• Incomplete fracture: bone breaks into two parts that 
are not completely
 separated.

Closed fracture: in which there is no break in the 
skin.
 Open fracture: there is a break in the skin in which 
broken bone protrude.

Comminuted fracture: in which the bone is broken 
and shattered into more than two fragments.
 Greenstick fracture: the bone is partially bent and
partially broken

Manifestations 
Pain    (from mild to severe pain)
Its severity depends on type of fracture, the bone(s) 
involved, and the amount of displacement.
Tenderness 
Deformity of the bone
Ecchymoses
Crepitation with any movement, and loss of 
function.

Management 
Immediate immobilization (Splint or cast)of the part 
to prevent unnecessary damage. Immobilized in the 
position in which it is found at the time of injury.

The limb should be supported firmly so that 
it will not be jarred when the patient is being 
moved.

Ice in a plastic bag be applied to the fracture area to help 
minimize swelling, and decrease pain
In open fracture , prophylactic antibiotics are usually 
administered

Surgical
 
management
 
 Used for severe fractures where an incision 
is made access the bone fragments, often 
combined with Internal Fixation  which uses 
screws, plates to stabilize the bone.

Traction
Skin traction Skeletal  traction

Nursing managements 
6 P  Assessment

• Put patient in a high Fowler's position
• Establish a peripheral IV fluids.
• Elevation of the affected part to prevent swelling , toes 
should be higher than the trunk.

If a cast is in place, be bivalved (split through all layers of 
the material). Dressings will be cut or replaced

Wash hands
Use aseptic technique when caring for wound
Report excessive drainage or inflammation to physician 
Culture drainage
Frequent monitoring of temperature
