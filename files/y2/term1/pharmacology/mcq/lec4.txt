Part II: Essay Hints 
1. Agonist: High affinity + High intrinsic activity (+1). Antagonist: High affinity + Zero 
activity (0). Partial Agonist: Affinity + Less activity (<1). Inverse Agonist: Affinity + 
Opposite activity (0 to -1). 
2. Ion Channels: Membrane, Direct coupling, e.g., Nicotinic. G-Protein: Membrane, 
G-proteins, e.g., Adrenergic. Enzymatic: Membrane, Direct enzyme, e.g., Insulin. 
Nuclear: Intracellular, DNA/Gene, e.g., Steroids. 
3. Up-regulation: Chronic antagonist use increases receptor number/sensitivity. 
Sudden stoppage leads to a massive response to natural agonists (e.g., 
catecholamines), causing tachycardia or angina in patients stopping Propranolol. 
4. Down-regulation: Chronic agonist use (Salbutamol) decreases receptor 
number/sensitivity, leading to a decreased drug effect in asthmatic patients.
