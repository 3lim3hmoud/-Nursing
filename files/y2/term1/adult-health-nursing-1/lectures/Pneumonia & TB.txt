1- Community-Acquired Pneumonia   
 
❖ Occurs either in the community setting or 
within the first 48 hours after hospitalization.

2-  Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia  
 
❖ Nosocomial pneumonia 
 The onset of pneumonia symptoms more than 48 
hours after admission  
Ventilator associated pneumonia (endotracheal 
intubation and mechanical ventilation.

3- Aspiration pneumonia  
❖ Aspiration pneumonia is an infection of the lung 
tissue (parenchyma) that develops after foreign 
material from the mouth or stomach is inhaled into 
the lungs 
It occurs when the material is not swallowed 
properly and "goes down the wrong pipe". 
Aspiration pneumonia may occur in the community 
or hospital setting.

• Supine positioning

Assessment & 
 Diagnostic Findings 
Physical examination:  
 Presence of symptoms. 
Diagnostic studies : 
 Chest X-ray 
 Sputum examination 
 Blood culture

Vaccination against pneumococcal infection for: 
• People 65 years of age or older 
• Immunocompromising people  
• People with functional or anatomic asplenia 
• People living in environments or social settings in 
which the risk of disease is high. 
• Immunocompetent people who are at increased risk 
for illness because of chronic illness  
Prevention

• Promote coughing and expectoration of  
Secretions 
• Initiate suctioning and chest physiotherapy . 
• Encourage smoking cessation. 
• Reposition frequently to promote lung 
expansion , elevate head of bed at least 30 
degrees. 
• Promote frequent oral hygiene.

Treatment  
 Medical  
•   Antibiotics are indicated with a viral respiratory 
infection only if bronchitis, or rhino sinusitis is 
present. 
• Hydration is a necessary part of therapy 
because fever and tachypnea may result in 
insensible fluid losses

• Antipyretics to treat headaches and fever 
• Warm, moist inhalations are helpful in relieving 
bronchial irritation 
 
• Antihistamines to reduced 
    sneezing and rhinorrhea 
• Oxygen therapy if hypoxemia develops

• Clinical manifestations of pneumonia: fatigue, 
tachypnea, use of accessory muscles for 
breathing, coughing, and purulent sputum.  
 
• Assess elderly patients for unusual behavior, 
altered mental status, dehydration, excessive 
fatigue. 
 
Nursing process for patients with pneumonia 
Assessment

• Ineffective airway clearance related to secretions 
• Activity intolerance related to impaired respiratory 
function 
• Risk for deficient fluid volume related to fever and a 
rapid respiratory rate. 
• Deficient knowledge about the treatment regimen 
and preventive health measures. 
Nursing Diagnoses

Improved airway patency 
Conserve energy 
Maintenance of proper fluid 
volume 
Understanding of the treatment and 
preventive measures 
Planning and goals

Improving airway patience 
• Deep breathing & coughing exercises , using incentive 
spirometer. 
• Correct positioning for lung expansion. 
• Chest physiotherapy (percussion , vibration and postural 
drainage). 
• Humidification may be used to loosen secretions and 
improve ventilation. 
Nursing interventions

Conserving energy 
• Promote rest  
• Moderate activity 
                           Promoting fluid intake 
• Encourage increased fluid intake (at least 2 
L/day), unless contraindicated as in heart failure

Promoting patients’ knowledge 
• Inform the patient and family about the cause, 
symptoms, management of pneumonia and the need 
for follow up.  
• If the patient is hospitalized, is instructed about the 
importance of adhering management during and 
after the hospital stay 
• Explanations should be given simply and in language 
that the patient can understand. 
• Written instructions should be provided.

• Improved airway patency 
• Rests and conserves energy  
• Maintains adequate hydration 
• Exhibits no complications

TB skin test (Mantoux test) 
• A dose of 0.1 ml of purified 
protein derivative (PPD) is 
injected intradermally into the 
forearm. 
•  After 48 to 72 hours, the 
injection site is examined for 
induration. 
•  If the induration measures 10 
mm or more, the test is 
considered positive.
