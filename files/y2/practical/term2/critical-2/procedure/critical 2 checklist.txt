 A. Nursing Preparation 
(Before the Administration) 
   
1.  Verify the physician’s TPN order,  
Perform hand hygiene and donning 
PPE. 
   
2.  Inspect the TPN bag for integrity, 
clarity, expiration, and label 
accuracy, and 
Allow the TPN solution to warm to 
room temperature (if refrigerated). 
   
3.  Assess the central line site and 
patency,  
Label a dedicated lumen for TPN 
use only. 
   
4.  Gather equipment: infusion pump, 
IV tubing, inline filter, alcohol 
wipes. 
   
5.  Prime the tubing with the TPN 
solution using aseptic technique. 
   
6.  Connect tubing to central line using 
sterile technique ,and  
Start the infusion at the prescribed 
rate using an infusion pump. 
   
7.  Monitor blood glucose every 4–6 
hours ,and  
Monitor for complications: 
infection, fluid overload, 
electrolyte imbalance, refeeding 
syndrome. 
   
8.  Assess and document central line 
site every shift. 
   
9.  Check and record intake/output and 
daily weight. 
Review daily labs: electrolytes, 
liver/renal function, glucose, 
triglycerides. 
   
10.  Document all interventions, 
assessments, and patient 
responses. 
If discontinuing TPN, taper 
gradually and flush the line
