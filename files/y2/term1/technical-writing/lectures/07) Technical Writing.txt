Shift Nursing Reports
❑ It is an essential document utilized by nursing staff to facilitate 
seamless communication between shifts. 
❑ It serves as a comprehensive summary of patient status, 
ongoing care plans, recent interventions, and any critical 
observations that need attention from incoming nurses. 
❑ The primary goal of this report is to ensure:
✓ Continuity of care, 
✓ minimize errors, and 
✓ promote patient safety..

4
Shift Nursing Reports
❑ As healthcare environments become increasingly complex, the 
importance of an organized and detailed shift report sheet 
cannot be overstated. 
❑ It acts as a bridge that:
✓ Connects outgoing and incoming nursing teams, 
✓ Ensuring that vital information is accurately transferred, and
✓ Patient care remains uninterrupted and of high quality..

Nursing Shift Report (Case Study)
Nurse Sarah Ahmed, a registered nurse working the 07:00 to 19:00 day 
shift on 22 November 2025, was assigned to care for Hassan 
Mohamed, a 57-year-old male admitted to Room 214, Bed B. Hassan 
had been admitted the previous evening with a primary diagnosis of 
community-acquired pneumonia, complicated by acute hypoxia. His 
allergy to penicillin had been noted in his chart, as well as his Full 
Code status.
When Sarah began her shift, she assessed Hassan’s condition. His vital 
signs showed a temperature of 37.8°C, heart rate of 102 bpm, 
respiratory rate of 22/min, blood pressure of 126/78 mmHg, and 
oxygen saturation of 92% while receiving 2 L/min of oxygen via 
nasal cannula. Hassan reported mild chest pain (3/10) that worsened 
with coughing. He was alert and oriented, cooperative, and able to 
ambulate with help, though he fatigued quickly.

14
Nursing Shift Report (Case Study)
Throughout the shift, Sarah administered the patient’s prescribed 
medications, including IV Ceftriaxone at 08:00, IV Azithromycin at 
10:00, oral Paracetamol at 11:45, and two Salbutamol nebulizer 
treatments. She also performed chest physiotherapy twice and 
encouraged the use of the incentive spirometer every hour. By midday, 
Hassan showed increased productive coughing with yellow sputum 
and experienced mild shortness of breath on exertion, prompting 
Sarah to monitor him closely. She increased his oxygen flow, promoted 
fluid intake, and continued regular assessments for fever or worsening 
respiratory distress.
The care plan for the day included a scheduled chest X-ray at 17:00 
and a pending sputum culture. Hassan was placed on a soft, high-
protein diet and advised to drink over 2 liters of water daily. He was 
restricted from strenuous activity and instructed to ambulate only with 
assistance. Hassan expressed his goals for the day: to breathe more 
comfortably, reduce his coughing, and avoid another fever spike.

15
Nursing Shift Report (Case Study)
Sarah inspected Hassan’s IV cannula in his left forearm, which 
remained clean and patent with no signs of redness or swelling. His 
ongoing IV therapy included normal saline at 75 mL/hr, along with the 
scheduled IV antibiotics. He also received Enoxaparin 40 mg 
subcutaneously for DVT prevention.
During the shift, Hassan required continuous pulse oximetry, incentive 
spirometer use, and occasional nebulizer treatments. He had no 
wounds, and his respiratory support remained at 2 L/min of oxygen.
Discharge planning discussions indicated that Hassan might be 
medically stable for discharge between 24–25 November, depending on 
improvements in oxygenation. His wife visited at 13:00, and Sarah 
updated her regarding his progress. No financial or psychosocial 
concerns were identified, so no social work intervention was needed.

16
Nursing Shift Report (Case Study)
At the end of her shift, Sarah prepared the handover for the incoming 
nurse, Ali Mostafa. She highlighted important alerts such as monitoring 
for fever, reviewing the sputum culture and upcoming chest X-ray 
results, and ensuring that his oxygen saturation stayed above 92%. 
Pending tasks included assessment of the IV site later in the evening and 
administering nebulizer treatments as needed.
Sarah signed off her report at 18:45, completing her responsibilities for 
the day and transferring care safely to Ali.
