2. First Three Microscopic (Cytologic) Criteria of Malignancy 
1. Increased nucleus-to-cytoplasm (N/C) ratio 
2. Hyperchromatism (darkly stained nuclei) 
3. Pleomorphism (variation in size and shape of cells and nuclei) 
 
3. Microscopic Picture of Leiomyoma 
• Composed of interlacing bundles of smooth muscle fibers cut in different 
directions. 
• Cells are long and spindle-shaped. 
• Nuclei are centrally located, blunt-ended, and cigar-shaped. 
• Cytoplasm is abundant and eosinophilic.

1. Direct spread (local invasion). 
2. Lymphatic spread. 
3. Vascular (hematogenous) spread. 
4. Transcoelomic spread (e.g., Krukenberg tumor). 
5. Perineural spread.
