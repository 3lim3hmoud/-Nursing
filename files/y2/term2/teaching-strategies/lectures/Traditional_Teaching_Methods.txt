DEFINITION
Traditional Teaching
Also known as lecture-based or didactic approach - a
method where the teacher transmits knowledge
through lectures, readings, or other forms of
presentation.

1 LECTURE METHOD
Teacher delivers information verbally to students
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Efficient for large volumes of information
• Cost-effective for many students
• Systematic curriculum coverage
• Can inspire through expert delivery
LIMITATIONS
• Promotes passive learning
• Difficult for different learning styles
• Poor information retention
• Limited immediate feedback

2 GROUP DISCUSSION
Cooperative problem-solving with consensus
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Develops critical thinking
• Encourages active participation
• Builds communication skills
• Promotes collaborative learning
LIMITATIONS
• Time-consuming process
• May be dominated by few students
• Requires skilled facilitation
• Difficult to assess individual learning

3 DEMONSTRATION
Teacher shows skill/process for students to replicate
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Makes concepts concrete
• Effective for practical skills
• Engages visual learners
• Reduces student errors
LIMITATIONS
• Students remain passive observers
• Visibility issues in large classes
• Difficult to scale
• No guarantee of replication ability

4 RECITATION
Teacher questions, students recall learned material
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Reinforces memorization
• Provides immediate feedback
• Encourages participation
• Quick comprehension assessment
LIMITATIONS
• Focuses on rote memorization
• Can create student anxiety
• Limited critical thinking
• Teacher-dominated process

5 DRILL & PRACTICE
Repeated practice until skill mastery
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Builds skill automaticity
• Structured practice opportunities
• Develops student confidence
• Individual pacing possible
LIMITATIONS
• Can become tedious
• No higher-order thinking
• Surface learning only
• Poor transfer to novel contexts

6 TEXTBOOK METHOD
Textbook as primary curriculum guide
✓ ADVANTAGES
• Structured comprehensive curriculum
• Supports self-study
• Standardization across schools
• Convenient reference material
LIMITATIONS
• Rigid and disconnected
• Content becomes outdated
• Surface-level reading
• Reduces teacher creativity

TRADITIONAL vs 
MODERN
TRADITIONAL MODERN
Teacher-centered vs Student-centered
Authority source vs Facilitator guide
Passive recipient vs Active constructor
Tests & exams vs Portfolio & projects
Fixed vs Flexible

CONCLUSION
Effective teaching draws on both traditional and 
modern methods.
The question is not which is 'correct,' but which is 
most appropriate for the learning objective, 
student population, and context.
