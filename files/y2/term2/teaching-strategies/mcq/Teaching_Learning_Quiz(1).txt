Critical Thinking Scenario 1 30 points
Scenario:
A new teacher notices that despite using traditional lecture methods, students show low 
engagement and poor learning outcomes. The teacher has access to technology, but the 
learning environment is not conducive to interactive learning. The curriculum is rigid and 
doesn't allow much flexibility.
Critical Analysis Required:
1. Identify which component(s) of the education process need improvement
2. Analyze the relationship between teaching methods and learning outcomes in this scenario
3. Propose a comprehensive solution considering all four components
4. Explain how your solution aligns with the Teaching-Learning Cycle

Critical Thinking Scenario 2 30 points
Scenario:

with strong subject matter competency, but student feedback indicates poor communication 
and lack of engagement. The institution has limited resources for professional development.
Critical Analysis Required:
1. Evaluate which teacher competencies are lacking based on the scenario
2. Analyze the factors affecting the teaching-learning relationship
3. Design a cost-effective professional development plan
