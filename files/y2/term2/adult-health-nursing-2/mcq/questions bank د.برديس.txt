 17- List nursing interventions for aura phase of seizure  
 Observe for early warning signs (e.g., dizziness, unusual smells, anxiety, visual changes)  
 Help the patient sit or lie down in a safe place  
 Remove nearby dangerous objects (sharp or hard items)  
 Loosen tight clothing, especially around the neck  
 Stay with the patient (do not leave them alone)  
 Prepare emergency equipment (oxygen, suction)  
 Provide reassurance and reduce anxiety 
 
18- List nursing interventions during seizure  
 
 Ensure patient safety first  
  Place the patient in a side-lying position (recovery position) to prevent aspiration 
 Clear the area of harmful objects  
  Do NOT restrain the patient  
  Do NOT put anything in the patient’s mouth  
 Time the duration of the seizure  
 Monitor breathing and skin color (watch for cyanosis)  
  Do not give food or fluids during the seizure 
 
19- List nursing interventions after seizure  
 Maintain airway and keep the patient in the side-lying position  
 Check vital signs (pulse, respiration, blood pressure)  
 Assess level of consciousness (confusion and drowsiness are common)

 Reassure and reorient the patient  
 Check for injuries (e.g., tongue biting, head trauma)  
 Allow the patient to rest or sleep  
 Document: Duration of seizure ,Type of movements ,Presence of aura  
 Administer prescribed medications if ordered, such as Diazepam  
 Clear secretions if needed (suction if necessary)
