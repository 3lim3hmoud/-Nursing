Definition of Burn
Burns are tissue damage that results
from heat, overexposure to the sun or
other radiation, chemical or electrical 
contact.

Severity of Burn
Minor Burn Injury:
Second-degree burn of 
less than 15% (TBSA) in 
adults or less than 10% 
TBSA in children.
Third-degree burn of less 
than 2% TBSA not 
involving special rare areas 
(eyes, ears, face, hands, 
feet, perineum, joints).
Excludes: electrical 
injury, inhalation 
injury, concurrent 
trauma, all poor-risk 
patients (e.g., extremes 
of age, concurrent 
disease).

Moderate, Uncomplicated Burn Injury:
• Second-degree: burns of 15%—25% TBSA in adults or 10%-

• Third-degree: burns of less than 10% TBSA not involving 
special care areas.
• Excludes: electrical injury, inhalation injury, concurrent 
trauma, all poor-risk patients (e.g., extremes of age, 
concurrent disease).

Pathophysiology of burn
Burn injury is a result of heat transfer from one site to another. Tissue 
destruction results from coagulation, or protein denaturation
Burns that do not exceed 20% TBSA produce a primarily local 
response
Burns that exceed 20% TBSA may produce both a local and a 
systemic response

9. Increase mobility and participation in activities of daily living

▪ This phase begins 48 to 72 hours after burn injury
▪ During this phase, attention is directed toward 
continues assessment and maintenance of 
respiratory and circulatory status, fluid and 
electrolyte balance and gastrointestinal function.
▪ Also, infection control, burn wound care; pain 
management and nutritional support.

Rehabilitation begins immediately after the burn 
has occurred as early as the emergent period and 
often extends for years after injury. The priorities of 
this phase include wound healing, psychosocial 
support and restoring maximal functional activity.
