Medical Management
Treatment of CHF aims to relieve symptoms, maintain (normal fluid 
level in the circulatory system)
Improving prognosis by delaying progression of 
heart failure 
Reducing cardiovascular risk.

Diuretics: to increase the rate of urine production and the
removal of excessive extracellular fluids. The most
common types are:
• Thiazide diuretic
• Loop diuretic
• Potassium sparing diuretics.

Thiazide diuretics as Zaroxolyn, which 
increases potassium and bicarbonate 
execration.  It used with mild fluid overload

Loop diuretics as Lasix is used with more
sever fluid overload or with renal
insufficiency.

Spironalactone as Aldactone that inhabits
sodium reabsorption in the late distal tubule
and collecting duct.

Digitalis or digoxin. 
• It increases the force of myocardial contraction. 
• It enhances diuresis to relive edema.
