Part II: Essay Hints 
1. Additive: 1+1=2 (e.g., Ibuprofen + Paracetamol). Potentiation: 1+0 > 1 (e.g., 
Levodopa + Carbidopa). Synergism: 1+1 > 2 (e.g., Co-trimoxazole). 
2. Physiological Antagonism: Two drugs act at different receptors/mechanisms on 
the same system to produce opposite effects. Examples: Insulin/Glucagon on 
blood sugar; Adrenaline/Histamine on bronchi. 
3. Competitive: Binds same site, reversible, overcome by more agonist, rightward 
parallel shift of DRC. Noncompetitive: Binds different site, not overcome by more 
agonist, flattening of DRC. 
4. Adrenaline/Histamine: Histamine causes bronchoconstriction via histamine 
receptors. Adrenaline causes bronchodilatation via adrenergic β2 receptors, 
reversing the spasm through a different pathway.
