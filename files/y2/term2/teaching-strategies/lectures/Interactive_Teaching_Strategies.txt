Strategy 01  |  Think-Pair-Share (TPS)
Dr. Saeed Mohammad  |  Interactive Teaching Strategies
HOW IT WORKS
Students think individually, discuss
with a partner, then share with the
class.
• Pose an open clinical question
• Think silently (1–3 min)
• Discuss in pairs (3–5 min)
• Share with the whole class
• Instructor consolidates answers

Strategy 02  |  Case-Based Learning (CBL)
Dr. Saeed Mohammad  |  Interactive Teaching Strategies
HOW IT WORKS
Students analyze a real patient case to
apply knowledge and make clinical
decisions.
• Present a realistic patient case
• Identify nursing problems & gaps
• Groups discuss care plan
• Present reasoning to class
• Debrief & link to evidence

Strategy 06  |  Socratic Questioning
Dr. Saeed Mohammad  |  Interactive Teaching Strategies
HOW IT WORKS
The instructor guides students to
discover knowledge through structured
probing dialogue.
• Start with an observable clinical
finding
• Ask: "Why?", "What evidence?"
• Invite peer challenge
• Clarify assumptions & implications
• Guide to self-correction

Key Takeaways
1 Match strategy to objective  — Select based on Bloom's level — not habit.
2 Nursing context is non-negotiable  — Every strategy must be grounded in real clinical 
scenarios.
3 Feedback is the engine  — Interactive strategies only work with immediate, specific 
feedback.
4 Start small, scale up  — Introduce one strategy per course; refine before adding another.
5 Create psychological safety  — Wrong answers must be learning opportunities — not 
sources of shame.
