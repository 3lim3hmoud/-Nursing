1- Peptic ulcer
 Definition 
 Causes  
 Manifestations
 Diagnosis 
 Complications  
 Managements

Definition: Break in the continuity of the
esophageal, gastric, or duodenal mucosa. It occurs in
any part of gastrointestinal tract that comes in
contact with hydrochloric acid and pepsin .

 A major causative factor (60% of gastric and up to 90% of
duodenal ulcers) is chronic inflammation due to
Helicobacter pylori
 Emotional stress
 Drugs such as Aspirin and other NSAIDs
 Chronic gastritis
 Cigarettes smoking and using alcohol
 Overuse of laxatives is also known to cause peptic ulcers.
 A family history is often present in duodenal ulcers.
Causes

Assessment and diagnostic studies :
❖Endoscope: By direct visual identification, the location 
and severity of an ulcer can be described. 
❖Culture 
❖Biopsy
❖Stool antigen test.

Management 
Medical : 
•  Antacids ; neutralize gastric acid that reduce 
gastric acid. 
• Antibiotics
• In the absence of H. pylori, long-term higher 
dose of Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) is often 
used

Nursing process  
1- Assessment :
• Complete assessment of pain.
• Ask patient to list usual food intake for 72 hours to describe 
food habits as use of spicy foods, and caffeinated beverages 
and coffee.
• Assess vital signs and report tachycardia and hypotension as 
they may indicate anemia from GIT bleeding.

Nursing process  
2- Nursing diagnoses :
• Acute Pain related to increase gastric acid secretion
• Imbalanced nutrition related to changes in diet 
• Anxiety related to coping with an acute disease
• Knowledge deficit related to prevention of symptoms 
and management of the disease.
• High risk for bleeding as complications of peptic ulcer

Nursing process  
3- Nursing interventions : 
 goals : Relieve pain
• Avoid aspirin, foods and beverages that contain 
caffeine as coffee and tea.
• Follow regular meals.
• Administer medication as prescribed

Nursing process  
Dietary modification to avoid over secretion of acid by:
✓ Avoid alcohol, coffee, smoking and other caffeinated 
beverage. 
✓ Regular eating pattern to neutralize acid.
✓ Small frequent diet.
✓ Eat foods that can be tolerated and avoid those that 
produce pain.

Nursing process  
Dietary modification 
– During meal: semi fowler position 
– After meal rest for 20-30 minutes.
– Fluids are discouraged with meals but may be given 
1 hour before or 1 hour after mealtime.
– Dietary supplements as vitamin B12 and iron may 
be prescribed

Nursing process  
goals : Reduce anxiety 
– Allow patient to express feeling.
– Explanation about diagnosis, treatment and  
medication.
– Good interaction between patient and nurse.
– Encourage the family to participate in patient care 
and emotional support

Nursing process  
goals : Patient teaching
• Instruct patient about importance of continuing taking 
medication even after signs and symptoms have decreased.
• Avoid  smoking
• Rest and stress reduction.
• Awareness of complications 
• Follow up care

Nursing process  
goals : Monitoring and managing complications
• Assess patient for bleeding
•  Monitor vital signs
•  Monitor hemoglobin 
• Record hourly urinary output to detect anuria or 
oliguria.

Nursing process  
goals : Monitoring and managing complications
• Rapidly replace the blood lost.
• Insert urinary catheter to monitor urinary out put
• Administer O2 therapy.
• Place patient in supine position with legs elevated 
to prevent hypotension.
