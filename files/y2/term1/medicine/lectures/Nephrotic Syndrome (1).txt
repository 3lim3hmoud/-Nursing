Diagnostic Workup
ŌĆó Urinalysis: 3+ or greater proteinuria on dipstick; 24-hour 
urine protein quantification
ŌĆó Blood tests: serum albumin (low), cholesterol (high), 
kidney function tests
ŌĆó Kidney biopsy if diagnosis unclear or to guide treatment

Treatment Overview
ŌĆó First-line: corticosteroids (e.g., prednisone) to reduce 
inflammation
ŌĆó Diuretics to manage edema
ŌĆó Antihypertensives for blood pressure control
ŌĆó Lipid-lowering agents for hyperlipidemia
ŌĆó Monitor and prevent infections due to immunosuppression

Nursing Care Priorities
ŌĆó Monitor daily weights and strict intake/output to assess 
fluid balance
ŌĆó Assess for signs of infection (fever, lethargy, abdominal 
pain)
ŌĆó Observe for respiratory distress from fluid overload or 
ascites
ŌĆó Educate patient/family on medication adherence and 
infection prevention
ŌĆó Support nutrition and monitor for fatigue and appetite 
changes

Complications to Watch For
ŌĆó Increased risk of serious infections (peritonitis, respiratory 
infections)
ŌĆó Thromboembolism due to loss of anticoagulant proteins
ŌĆó Relapse of nephrotic syndrome requiring long-term 
steroid therapy
ŌĆó Potential progression to chronic kidney disease

Summary & Takeaway
ŌĆó Nephrotic syndrome is a complex kidney disorder with 
hallmark proteinuria and edema
ŌĆó Early recognition and treatment improve outcomes
ŌĆó Nurses play a critical role in monitoring, educating, and 
supporting patients
ŌĆó Stay vigilant for complications and advocate for
