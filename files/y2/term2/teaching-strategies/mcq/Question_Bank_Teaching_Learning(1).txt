PART I: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ) 
Choose the best answer for each question. (30 Questions) 
 
Section A — Easy (Questions 1–10) 
[Easy] 
Q1. Traditional teaching is also known as: 
     A. Progressive approach 
     B. Lecture-based or didactic approach 
     C. Discovery learning 
     D. Collaborative method 
Answer: B 
 
[Easy] 
Q2. The oldest and most universally recognized teaching method is: 
     A. Group Discussion 
     B. Demonstration 
     C. Lecture 
     D. Recitation 
Answer: C 
 
[Easy] 
Q3. Which of the following is a key characteristic of modern teaching 
strategies? 
     A. Teacher-centered instruction 
     B. Rote memorization 
     C. Student-centered active learning 
     D. Standardized textbook use 
Answer: C 
 
[Easy]

Q4. Learning is best defined as: 
     A. Receiving information from a teacher 
     B. A permanent change in knowledge, skills, or attitude 
     C. Attending classes regularly 
     D. Passing written examinations 
Answer: B 
 
[Easy] 
Q5. Education is described as: 
     A. A one-time event 
     B. Only classroom instruction 
     C. A systematic, sequential, logical process 
     D. Self-directed reading 
Answer: C 
 
[Easy] 
Q6. Drill and practice method is most effective for: 
     A. Building automaticity in foundational skills 
     B. Developing critical thinking 
     C. Creative expression 
     D. Group collaboration 
Answer: A 
 
[Easy] 
Q7. The demonstration method is particularly effective for: 
     A. Abstract theoretical concepts only 
     B. Large lecture halls 
     C. Teaching procedural and practical skills 
     D. Written examinations 
Answer: C

[Easy] 
Q8. In the flipped classroom model, students: 
     A. Attend lectures and take notes passively 
     B. Review content at home and apply it in class 
     C. Work individually throughout the session 
     D. Only use textbooks for learning 
Answer: B 
 
[Easy] 
Q9. Gamification in education involves: 
     A. Playing video games during class time 
     B. Applying game-design elements to boost motivation and engagement 
     C. Replacing exams with games 
     D. Using only digital tools for assessment 
Answer: B 
 
[Easy] 
Q10. Which component of education is considered the most important quality 
factor? 
     A. Curriculum 
     B. Learning environment 
     C. Teacher 
     D. Technology 
Answer: C

PART IV: Essay Questions 
Answer the following questions in complete sentences. Include examples 
where appropriate. 
 
A. List / Enumerate Questions 
[Easy] 
Q1. List FIVE key characteristics of traditional teaching methods. 
Model Answer Guidance: Expected: teacher -centered, lecture -based, 
curriculum-driven, standardized assessment, passive student role, 
emphasis on memorization. 
 
[Easy] 
Q2. Enumerate FOUR components of the education process and briefly 
define each one. 
Model Answer Guidance: Expected: Learning Environment, Teacher, 
Curriculum, Learner — each with a brief definition. 
 
[Moderate] 
Q3. List the SEVEN modern teaching strategies covered in this course and 
state the core focus of each. 
Model Answer Guidance: Expected: Active/Interactive Learning, 
Collaborative Learning, Flipped Classroom, Personalized Learning, PBL, 
Gamification, Blended Learning — with one-line description each. 
 
[Moderate] 
Q4. List SEVEN teacher competencies required for effective teaching and 
provide one example for each. 
Model Answer Guidance: Expected: Subject Matter, Transactional, 
Personal, Co-Curricular, Evaluation, Management, Community — each 
with an example.

[Difficult] 
Q5. Enumerate the steps of the Teaching -Learning Cycle and explain how 
each step contributes to effective education. 
Model Answer Guidance: Expected: Plan → Implement → Engage → 
Learn → Assess → Reflect → Improve — with explanation of each.

B. Explain Questions 
[Easy] 
Q1. Explain the main difference between traditional and modern teaching 
approaches in terms of the teacher's role. 
Model Answer Guidance: Traditional: teacher as sole authority/knowledge 
transmitter. Modern: teacher as facilitator, coach, guide who encourages 
student-led discovery. 
 
[Easy] 
Q2. Explain what is meant by 'learning' and describe TWO factors that 
influence it. 
Model Answer Guidance: Learning = permanent change in 
capability/behavior. Factors: prior knowledge, motivation, environment, 
teaching quality, learner readiness. 
 
[Moderate] 
Q3. Explain the concept of Problem-Based Learning (PBL) and describe how 
it is applied in nursing education. 
Model Answer Guidance: PBL = inquiry -driven strategy using real -world 
problems. In nursing: clinical case studies, diagnostic reasoning, ethical 
decisions, NANDA/NIC/NOC application, interprofessional 
communication. 
 
[Moderate] 
Q4. Explain TWO advantages and TWO limitations of the lecture method. 
Model Answer Guidance: Advantages: cost -effective for large groups, 
efficient content delivery. Limitations: attention declines after 20 min, 
passive learning, does not accommodate all styles, limited feedback.

[Difficult] 
Q5. Explain how the flipped classroom model transforms the educational 
experience for both teacher and student, using specific examples from 
nursing education. 
Model Answer Guidance: Teacher shifts from lecturer to coach; student 
becomes active agent. Examples: pre -class video on electrolytes + in -
class case: Na 118, K 6.5 — apply clinical reasoning. Deeper 
engagement, critical thinking, peer collaboration.

C. Discuss Questions 
[Moderate] 
Q1. Discuss the role of feedback in interactive teaching strategies. Why is 
immediate and specific feedback essential for student learning? 
Model Answer Guidance: Feedback as the engine of learning; 
gamification example (Kahoot correction of wrong antibiotic choice); 
simulation debriefing; psychological safety; error as learning opportunity. 
 
[Moderate] 
Q2. Discuss the importance of collaborative and cooperative learning in 
nursing education. How does teamwork in the classroom prepare students 
for the clinical environment? 
Model Answer Guidance: Nursing = teamwork essential for patient safety; 
Group Projects simulate multidisciplinary teams; Learning Stations; 
Jigsaw builds accountability; mirrors interprofessional collaboration in 
healthcare. 
 
[Difficult] 
Q3. Compare and contrast traditional teaching methods with modern 
pedagogical approaches. In your discussion, address: philosophical 
differences, strengths and limitations, and contextual appropriateness. 
Model Answer Guidance: Philosophy: teacher -centered vs student -
centered; Strengths of traditional: structured, efficient, cost -effective. 
Modern: develops critical thinking, clinical reasoning, collaboration. Each 
appropriate in context; effective teachers integrate both.
