Lecture 01 · Neurology
Head Injury
& Raised ICP
Traumatic Brain Injury — Assessment, Monitoring & Management
إصابة الرأس وارتفاع الضغط داخل الجمجمة — التقييم والمراقبة والعلاج
Definition
What is Head Injury?
ما هي إصابة الرأس؟
  • Trauma to skull → mild to extensive brain damage
  • Immediate complications: cerebral bleeding, hematomas
  • Uncontrolled raised ICP, infections, seizures
  • Changes in personality, cranial nerve deficits
Classification · التصنيف
Types of Head Injury
OPEN CLOSED
  • Open: Scalp lacerations, skull fractures, dura mater breach
  • Concussion: Temporary LOC — no structural damage
  • Contusion: Bruising ± subdural/extradural blood
  • Epidural Hematoma: Arterial bleed — SURGICAL EMERGENCY
Epidural · فوق الجافية
Epidural Hematoma
  • Between dura & skull
  • Arterial — rapid formation
  • Meningeal artery tear
  • ⚠️ Surgical emergency
Subdural · تحت الجافية
Subdural Hematoma
  • Below dura — slow forming
  • Venous bleed
  • Tears in bridging veins
  • Chronic or acute forms
SAH / ICH · نزيف
Other Bleeds
  • ICH: Multiple hemorrhages around contused area
  • SAH: Into ventricles/subarachnoid space

Key Formula · المعادلة الأساسية
CPP = MAP − ICP
الضغط التروي للدماغ = الضغط الشرياني — ضغط الجمجمة
Target CPP > 70 mmHg
Cerebral Blood Flow 50 cc/100g/min
ICP Monitoring Indication GCS < 8 + CT
ICP Monitoring Devices · أجهزة القياس
Monitor Options
  • Intraventricular catheter (ventriculostomy) — gold standard
  • Subarachnoid bolt
  • Epidural or subdural catheter
  • Fiberoptic transducer-tipped catheter
⚠️ Lumbar puncture CONTRAINDICATED in raised ICP — risk of herniation
Preemptive Treatment · الإجراءات الوقائية
HOB 30° PaCO₂ 35–40 Euvolemia Seizure Prophylaxis Sedation CPP >70
Active ICP Treatment · العلاج الفعلي
CSF Drainage Hyperventilate PaCO₂ 30–35 Mannitol 0.5–1g/kg Hypertonic Saline Patient Cooling Hemicraniectomy Barbiturate Coma
Clinical Manifestations of Raised ICP · المظاهر السريرية
↓ LOC Pupillary Changes Cushing's Triad Papilledema Decortication Decerebration Seizures CSF Drainage from Ears/Nose Headache (↑ morning) Vomiting Nuchal Rigidity Babinski Sign
Nursing Interventions · التدخلات التمريضية
  • Monitor respiratory status — patent airway (↑CO₂ = ↑edema)
  • Monitor neurological status and vital signs including temp
  • Maintain head elevation — reduce venous pressure
  • Prevent neck flexion
  • Initiate normothermia measures
  • Avoid morphine sulfate — respiratory depressant, ↑ICP
  • Monitor drainage from nose/ears — may be CSF
  • Do NOT suction or clean nose if drainage noted
  • Instruct patient to avoid coughing — ↑ICP
  • Initiate seizure precautions

Lecture 02 · Cardiovascular
Hemodynamic
Monitoring
Pressure · Flow · Oxygenation within the Cardiovascular System
المراقبة الديناميكية الدموية — الضغط والتدفق وتأكسج الدم في الجهاز القلبي الوعائي
Definition · التعريف
Hemodynamic Monitoring
قياس الضغط والتدفق والأكسجة داخل الجهاز القلبي الوعائي
  • Measurement of pressure, flow and oxygenation within the cardiovascular system
  • Essential for critically ill patients in the ICU
  • Guides fluid resuscitation and vasoactive drug therapy

Indirect BP · قياس الضغط غير المباشر
Sphygmomanometer Methods
  • Auscultatory: Mercury manometer + stethoscope; listen for Korotkoff sounds
  • Oscillometric: Inflated cuff detects pulsatile pressure changes automatically
⚠️ Cuff Sizing Matters · حجم الكفة مهم
Too small → False HIGH reading
Too large → False LOW reading
Invasive BP Monitoring · مراقبة الضغط الغازية
Direct Arterial Line
  • Continuous real-time BP monitoring
  • Arterial sites: Radial (preferred), Femoral, Brachial, Dorsalis pedis
  • Equipment: Transducer + amplifier + flush system + stopcocks
🔧 Zeroing required — confirms monitor reads zero when no pressure applied
Central Venous Pressure · الضغط الوريدي المركزي
CVP Values · قيم الضغط
Normal CVP 2–6 mmHg / 2–8 cmH₂O
High CVP (>7) Volume overload / HF
Low CVP Hypovolemic Shock
High CVP Causes · أسباب الارتفاع
Right Heart Failure Biventricular Failure Pericardial Disease Pneumothorax Pulmonary Stenosis
Indications for CVC · مؤشرات القسطرة
  • IV fluids & electrolytes
  • Drug therapy (vasopressors)
  • CVP monitoring
  • Parenteral nutrition
  • Transvenous pacemaker
  • Blood products
  • No peripheral access
Pulmonary Artery Catheter · قسطرة الشريان الرئوي
Swan-Ganz Catheter
  • Balloon port — inflates to float catheter into PA
  • Thermistor port — measures temperature for CO
  • CVP port — measures right atrial pressure
  • PA port — measures PA systolic/diastolic/PCWP
  • Thermodilution technique for Cardiac Output
Jugular Venous Pressure · الضغط الوداجي
JVP Measurement
  • Patient at 45° — inspect internal jugular pulsation
  • Normal JVP: ~8 cm blood (3 cm above sternal angle)
  • Measure vertical distance from sternal angle to top of pulsation
  • Add 5 cm to give true JVP
Phlebostatic axis = reference point for transducer leveling
Shock Classification — Hemodynamic Monitoring Helps Distinguish · تصنيف الصدمة
Cardiogenic — ↑CVP, ↓CO Hypovolemic — ↓CVP, ↓CO Distributive (Septic) — ↓SVR, ↑CO Obstructive — ↑CVP, ↓CO

Lecture 03 · Gastroenterology
GIT Bleeding
Upper & Lower Gastrointestinal Hemorrhage — Causes, Assessment & Management
النزيف الهضمي العلوي والسفلي — الأسباب والتقييم والعلاج
Upper GIB · النزيف الهضمي العلوي
Above Ligament of Treitz
فوق رباط Treitz عند مرفق الاثني عشر
Frequency vs Lower GIB 4× more common
Mortality (hospitalized) 5–10%
GIT Emergency MOST COMMON
Lower GIB · النزيف الهضمي السفلي
Below Ligament of Treitz
أسفل من رباط Treitz — الصائم، اللفائفي، القولون، المستقيم
  • Presents as hematochezia (bright red blood) or melena
  • Sources: Jejunum, ileum, colon, rectum, anus
  • Major causes: diverticular disease, enterocolitis, AVM
Causes of Upper GI Bleeding · أسباب النزيف العلوي
Common · شائع
Peptic Ulcer Erosion Esophagitis Gastritis Duodenitis Esophageal Varices Mallory-Weiss Tear Tumors Bleeding Tendency
Rare · نادر
Aorto-enteric Fistula Dieulafoy's Disease
⚠️ Classify as variceal vs non-variceal — management differs!
تمييز الدوالي عن غير الدوالي — العلاج مختلف
Peptic Ulcer · قرحة هضمية
Peptic Ulcer Disease
  • Imbalance: acid-pepsin vs mucosal defense
  • Duodenal ulcers: 4× more than gastric; more likely to bleed
Aetiology:
H. pylori NSAIDs Smoking
Esophageal Varices · دوالي المريء
Portal Hypertension
  • ↑Portal resistance → collateral vessels
  • Portosystemic shunting in distal esophagus & stomach
  • Located within 3–5 cm of gastro-esophageal junction
  • Variceal bleeding: often severe, recurrent
Mallory-Weiss · تمزق ملوري-فيس
Mallory-Weiss Tear
  • Longitudinal tear at gastro-esophageal junction
  • Caused by repetitive forceful vomiting
  • Usually stops spontaneously
  • Tx: epinephrine, cautery, clipping — rarely surgery
Clinical Presentation · المظاهر السريرية
Presentation
Hematemesis Melena Hematochezia Occult Blood Loss
Shock Signs
Hypotension Tachycardia Pallor Cold Extremities Orthostatic Dizziness
Basic Investigations · الفحوصات الأساسية
Full Blood Count Hb may be normal early
Urea & Electrolytes ↑Urea = severe bleed
LFTs Liver disease?
Prothrombin Time Anticoag/liver Dz
Crossmatch ≥ 2 units blood
Initial Resuscitation · الإنعاش الأولي
1
ABC — Airway, Breathing, Circulation + Vital Signs
2
Two wide-bore IV cannulas — IV crystalloid fluids to raise BP
3
Transfuse blood when actively bleeding with low BP & tachycardia
4
Oxygen supplementation for all patients in shock
Management Summary · ملخص العلاج
Variceal Bleeding · دوالي
IV Terlipressin Antibiotic Prophylaxis Emergency Endoscopy Band Ligation / Sclerotherapy TIPSS if refractory
Non-Variceal · غير دوالي
IV PPI (Omeprazole 80mg bolus) Endoscopic Therapy Adrenaline Injection Heater Probe / Clips
Surgery Indications · مؤشرات الجراحة
  • Failure of medical treatment & endoscopic hemostasis
  • Persistent hypotension
  • Coexisting condition (perforation, obstruction, malignancy)
Types: under-running ± pyloroplasty (duodenal), gastric under-run + biopsy, partial gastrectomy
Lower GIB Causes · أسباب النزيف السفلي
Diverticular Disease Ulcerative Colitis Crohn's Disease Ischemic Colitis Angiodysplasia AVM Polyps / Tumors Hemorrhoids Anal Fissure Meckel's Diverticulum
