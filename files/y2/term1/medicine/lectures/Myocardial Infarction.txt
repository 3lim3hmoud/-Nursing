What is Myocardial Infarction (MI)?
’üĄMI is the death of heart muscle tissue due to 
prolonged ischemia (lack of oxygen).
’üĄCaused mainly by coronary artery blockage from 
plaque rupture and thrombus formation.
’üĄ"Time is muscle": rapid intervention saves heart 
tissue and lives.

Pathophysiology: How MI Happens
’üĄCoronary arteries supply oxygen-rich 
blood to heart muscle; blockage causes 
ischemia.
’üĄPlaque rupture exposes thrombogenic 
material ŌåÆ rapid clot formation ŌåÆ artery 
occlusion.
’üĄMost MIs involve the left ventricle, 
especially the Left Anterior Descending 
artery (LAD).

Causes 
’üĄAtherosclerosis (most common): 
plaque rupture ŌåÆ thrombosis ŌåÆ 
occlusion
’üĄCoronary artery spasm
’üĄEmboli
’üĄSevere anemia or hypoxia (rare)

B. Risk Factors
’üĄModifiable: smoking, hypertension, 
diabetes, high cholesterol, obesity, 
sedentary lifestyle, stress
’üĄNon-modifiable: age, male sex, family 
history, genetic predisposition

STEMI (ST-Elevation MI)
’üĄComplete occlusion of a coronary 
artery
’üĄST elevation on ECG
’üĄRequires immediate reperfusion 
(PCI or thrombolytics)

NSTEMI (NonŌĆōST-Elevation MI):
’üĄPartial occlusion
’üĄNo ST elevation; elevated 
cardiac biomarkers
’üĄManaged with medication + 
possible PCI

Classic Signs & Symptoms
’üĄCrushing chest pain, often radiating to left 
arm, jaw, neck, or back.
’üĄShortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat, 
anxiety, and palpitations.
’üĄSome patients (especially women, elderly, 
diabetics) may have atypical or silent 
symptoms.

Diagnostic Tools for MI
’üĄElectrocardiogram (ECG): ST elevation 
(STEMI) or ST depression/T wave 
inversion (NSTEMI).
’üĄCardiac biomarkers: Troponin rise 
confirms myocardial injury.
’üĄAdditional tests: Echocardiogram, stress 
tests, coronary angiography.

Immediate Nursing Interventions:
’üĄMorphine: pain relief and anxiety reduction.
’üĄOxygen: improve oxygen supply if hypoxic.
’üĄNitrates: dilate coronary arteries to improve 
blood flow.
’üĄAspirin: prevent further clotting.
’üĄEstablish IV access and continuous cardiac 
monitoring.

Advanced Treatments & Procedures
’üĄPercutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI): 
balloon angioplasty and stent placement.
’üĄThrombolytic therapy: clot-busting drugs if PCI 
unavailable.
’üĄCoronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) for 
severe blockages.
’üĄGoal: reperfusion within 90 minutes of hospital 
arrival.

Complications of MI
’üĄArrhythmias (ventricular fibrillation, ventricular 
tachycardia)
’üĄHeart failure
’üĄCardiogenic shock
’üĄPapillary muscle rupture
’üĄVentricular aneurysm
’üĄPericarditis
’üĄSudden cardiac death

Nursing Assessment & Monitoring
’üĄFrequent vital signs and cardiac rhythm 
monitoring.
’üĄAssess pain characteristics and response to 
treatment.
’üĄWatch for complications: arrhythmias, 
cardiogenic shock, heart failure.
’üĄEmotional support and anxiety management.

Post-MI Care & Patient Education
’üĄMedication adherence: beta blockers, 
ACE inhibitors, statins, antiplatelets.
’üĄLifestyle changes: smoking cessation, 
diet, exercise, weight control.
’üĄRecognize warning signs of recurrent MI.
’üĄReferral to cardiac rehabilitation 
programs.

Summary & Key Takeaways
’üĄMI is a life-threatening emergency requiring 
rapid recognition and treatment.
’üĄNurses play a critical role in early assessment, 
intervention, and education.
’üĄUnderstanding MI pathophysiology and care 
protocols improves patient outcomes.
’üĄRemember: timely action saves heart muscle 
and lives.
