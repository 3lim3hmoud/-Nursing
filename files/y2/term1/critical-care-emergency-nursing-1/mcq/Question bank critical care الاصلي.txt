Case-Based / Application Questions 
 
Case 1: 
A 45-year-old man presents with severe vomiting and diarrhea for 
three days. BP: 85/50 mmHg, HR: 120 bpm, cold clammy skin, and 
urine output < 20 mL/hr. 
 
Identify the type of shock. 
 
Explain the underlying pathophysiology. 
 
Outline the immediate management. 
 
 
 
 
Answer: 
 
Type: Hypovolemic shock 
 
Pathophysiology: Loss of extracellular fluid → ↓ venous return → ↓ CO 
→ ↓ tissue perfusion

Management: IV crystalloids, oxygen therapy, monitor urine output, 
treat cause (rehydration) 
 
Case 2: 
A 65-year-old diabetic woman with pneumonia becomes hypotensive 
(BP 80/45 mmHg), febrile, and tachycardic. 
 
Identify the type of shock. 
 
Explain why vasodilation occurs. 
 
What is the first-line vasopressor in this condition? 
 
 
 
 
Answer: 
 
Type: Septic shock (distributive) 
 
Vasodilation due to release of endotoxins and cytokines → loss of 
vascular tone

First-line vasopressor: Norepinephrine 
 
Case 3: 
A patient after a spinal cord injury develops hypotension and 
bradycardia. Skin is warm and dry. 
 
What type of shock is this? 
 
Explain the mechanism of bradycardia. 
 
State the initial management. 
 
 
 
 
 
Answer: 
 
Type: Neurogenic shock 
 
Mechanism: Loss of sympathetic tone → unopposed vagal stimulation 
→ bradycardia

Management: Maintain airway, fluids, vasopressors, atropine for 
bradycardia 
 
Case 4: 
A patient develops sudden hypotension, dyspnea, and wheezing after 
receiving IV antibiotics. 
 
What type of shock is this? 
 
What is the drug of choice? 
 
Mention two other supportive treatments. 
 
 
 
 
Answer: 
 
Type: Anaphylactic shock 
 
Drug of choice: Epinephrine (IM/IV) 
 
Supportive: Oxygen therapy, IV fluids, antihistamines, corticosteroids

Chest trauma questions 
Q1 (MCQ): Which ribs are most commonly fractured in chest 
trauma? 
A) 1–3 
B) 5–9 
C) 10–12 
D) All ribs equally 
Answer: B) 5–9 
Rationale: The mid-thoracic ribs are most exposed and vulnerable to 
direct trauma. 
Q2 (MCQ): Fractures of the first three ribs are dangerous because 
they may injure: 
A) Heart 
B) Subclavian artery 
C) Liver 
D) Diaphragm 
Answer: B) Subclavian artery 
Rationale: These ribs are protected by muscles and clavicle; fracturing 
them usually requires significant force, risking injury to major vessels. 
Q3 (Short Answer): List common clinical manifestations of rib 
fractures. 
Answer: Pain, tenderness, bruising, crepitus, muscle spasm, shallow 
breathing. 
Q4 (True/False): Lower rib fractures can injure the spleen or liver. 
Answer: True 
Q5 (MCQ): Which nursing intervention is most important for rib 
fracture management?

A) Strict bed rest 
B) Encourage deep breathing and coughing 
C) Avoid analgesics 
D) Apply rigid chest binders 
Answer: B) Encourage deep breathing and coughing 
Rationale: Prevents atelectasis and pneumonia; rigid binding is no 
longer recommended. 
 
Q6 (MCQ): Flail chest is defined as: 
A) One rib fracture 
B) Three or more ribs fractured in two or more places 
C) Any rib fracture with lung contusion 
D) Rib fracture with hemothorax 
Answer: B) Three or more ribs fractured in two or more places 
Q7 (MCQ): Paradoxical chest wall motion is: 
A) Outward movement during inspiration 
B) Inward movement during inspiration 
C) Normal chest wall movement 
D) Chest expansion is equal 
Answer: B) Inward movement during inspiration 
Q8 (Short Answer): Name three complications of flail chest. 
Answer: Pulmonary contusion, atelectasis, respiratory compromise, 
hypoxemia, pneumonia. 
Q9 (MCQ): The most appropriate management of flail chest 
includes: 
A) Immediate surgery for all cases 
B) Pain control, oxygen therapy, mechanical ventilation if needed

C) Only chest binders 
D) Restriction of movement 
Answer: B) Pain control, oxygen therapy, mechanical ventilation if 
needed

Short Answer Questions 
31. Define cardiac arrest and explain why immediate 
intervention is necessary. 
Answer: Cardiac arrest is the sudden cessation of effective cardiac

activity. Immediate intervention is necessary to restore circulation 
and prevent brain injury or death. 
32. List at least 5 causes of cardiopulmonary arrest. 
Answer: Myocardial infarction, arrhythmias, respiratory failure, 
drowning, trauma. 
33. Explain the roles of at least three members of the code 
team. 
Answer: Leader – directs resuscitation; Compressor – performs 
chest compressions; Recorder – documents events; Airway 
provider – manages airway; IV/medication provider – administers 
drugs. 
34. Describe the steps to establish an airway and provide 
ventilations during CPR. 
Answer: Open airway using head-tilt/chin-lift or jaw-thrust, 
provide 2 rescue breaths after 30 compressions, ensure chest rises. 
35. Discuss the rationale for therapeutic hypothermia in post-
cardiac arrest patients. 
Answer: Cooling reduces metabolic demand and protects the brain 
from hypoxic injury. 
36. What are the main differences between defibrillation and 
cardioversion? 
Answer: Defibrillation is unsynchronized and used in 
VF/pulseless VT; cardioversion is synchronized and used for 
unstable arrhythmias with a pulse. 
37. Name three medications used during a code and their 
indications. 
Answer: Epinephrine – asystole/PEA; Amiodarone – VF/pulseless 
VT; Atropine – bradycardia.

38. Explain the importance of using a backboard during 
CPR. 
Answer: Provides a firm surface for effective chest compressions. 
39. Describe how to assess responsiveness in an unresponsive 
patient. 
Answer: Shake the patient gently and shout, “Are you okay?” 
40. List the signs of effective chest compressions during CPR. 
Answer: Chest rises and falls, palpable carotid pulse returns, 
improved skin color. 
41. Explain the steps to operate an AED. 
Answer: Turn on AED, attach pads, allow AED to analyze rhythm, 
deliver shock if advised, resume CPR. 
42. What precautions should be taken when defibrillating a 
patient with an implanted pacemaker? 
Answer: Place pads at least 1 inch away from pacemaker, follow 
standard shock protocol. 
43. Describe the post-resuscitation monitoring process. 
Answer: Monitor airway, breathing, circulation, cardiac rhythm, 
neurological status, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation. 
44. Explain the significance of early defibrillation in 
ventricular fibrillation. 
Answer: Early defibrillation increases the chance of restoring 
normal rhythm and survival. 
45. Describe the difference in CPR technique between adults, 
children, and infants. 
Answer: Adults – 2 hands, depth 2 inches; Children – 1–2 hands, 
depth 1.5 inches; Infants – 2 fingers, depth 1.5 inches.

46. Discuss the role of oxygen therapy during and after CPR. 
Answer: Provides oxygen to vital organs and prevents hypoxia 
during resuscitation. 
47. Explain how to manage a choking adult patient before 
cardiac arrest occurs. 
Answer: Perform Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts) until 
obstruction is cleared. 
48. Describe the steps to convert an irregular heart rhythm 
using synchronized cardioversion. 
Answer: Assess rhythm, apply pads, set energy level, synchronize 
with R wave, deliver shock while monitoring patient. 
49. List complications that can occur during CPR and how to 
minimize them. 
Answer: Rib fractures, pneumothorax, aspiration; minimize by 
proper technique and careful handling. 
50. Explain the importance of debriefing and documentation 
after a code. 
Answer: Debriefing improves team performance, documentation 
ensures accurate record, legal and quality assurance purposes.
