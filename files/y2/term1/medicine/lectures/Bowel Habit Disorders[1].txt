Definition :
frequent loose stool > 300gm / day.
[false diarrhea]  frequent stool < 300gm/day 
usually due to ↑ intestinal motility.

Mechanisms:
• Osmotic diarrhea: large non absorbed 
hypertonic substances in GIT lumen => ↑ fluid 
accumulation.
• Secretory diarrhea: results from active secretion 
of intestinal fluid and electrolytes and decrease 
absorption.
1- Enterotoxins e.g. cholera and E-coli .
2- Hormones as vasoactive intestinal 
polypeptide secreting tumours(VIPomas), 
gastrin and glucagon.

• Inflammatory diarrhea: Due to damage of 
int.mucosa => loss of fluid and blood and ↓ 
absorption of fluid and electrolytes as in :
– IBD as crohn's and ulcerative colitis
– Infection as shigellosis
• Abnormal intestinal motility: false diarrhea [↑ 
frequency but no ↑ in volume] as in 
1.DM.
2.Hyperthyroidism.
3.Post vagotomy.

Acute diarrhea:
• Dietary indiscretions (حماقةand unripe 
fruits.
• Infections as food poisoning, viral GE, 
cholera.
• Traveller,s diarrhea: E. coli, shigella or E. 
histolitica.

Chronic diarrhea:
• Parasitic, viral or Fungal infection
• IBD and IBS
• Malabsorption Gut resection
• Drugs
• Colonic tumours
• Endocrinal diarrhea: 
– VIPomas => more than 3 L/day and ↑ level of VIP. (pancreatic cholera)
– Gastrinoma => ↑ gastrin level in ZE syndrome
– Glucagonoma => ↑ glucagon level and ↑ blood glucose level.
– Carcinoid syndrome => ↑ urinary [5-HIAA].
– Medullary carcinoma of thyroid => ↑ level of calcitonin.
• Purgative abuse.
• Chronic idiopathic (pseudo-pancreatic cholera)

• Acute diarrhea:
– Fever, abd. Pain and vomiting usually present.
– Dehydration may occur in sever cases.
• Chronic diarrhea:
- CP of cause.

Acute diarrhea:  
Necessary if diarrhea prolonged > one week
• Stool examination  for ova, cysts, pus and 
parasites.
• Sigmoidoscopy, rectal biopsy may (rarely 
needed).
Chronic diarrhea:
• Investigations of the cause.
• Sigmoidoscopy, rectal biopsy.

Acute diarrhea:
• Fluid replacement entral or parentral according 
to the condition.
• Antibiotics for bacterial diarrhea.
• Antidiarrheal drugs may impair clearance of 
responsible pathogens as loperamide or 
codeine phosphate.
• Chronic diarrhea: treatment of the cause.

Definition 
• Defecation twice a week or less. 
Normal bowel habit vary from:
2-3 times / day to once every 2-3 days.

Aetiology:
• Physiological: Immobility,elderly, pregnancy or ↓fiber 
intake
• Intestinal obstruction as in tumours, obstructive 
hernias or volvolus.
• Colonic disorders as IBS or megacolon.
• Pain as in anal fissure or thrombosed haemorrhoids.

• Drugs as anticholinergics, iron, opiates ca-
channel blockers or diuretics.
• Immobility.
• Endocrinal and metabolic : diabetes mellitus, 
hypothyroidism, porphyria and 
hypercalcemia

Investigations:
• investigations for the cause.
• Sigmoidoscopy or colonoscopy and 
biopsy.
• Ano-rectal manometry  may be done.

Treatment
• Treatment of cause.
• Bulk forming laxatives [high fibre diet as fruits and 
vegetables contain non absorbable polysaccharides or 
methylcellulose  which form a bulk and st. int. motility ]
• Stimulant laxatives as bisacodyl or docusate sodium
• Osmotic laxatives as lactulose or magnesium sulphate

• Enema [docusate sodium or sodium 
citrate]may be needed in some cases.
• Suppositories as glycerol or bisacodyl
• Prokinetic agents: as mosapride, itopride, 
domperidone or metoclopramide.

• Prucalopride: 
Stimulate serotonin 5-HT4 
receptor ==> stimulates colonic 
mass movements, which provide 
the main propulsive force for 
defecation.

• Lubiprostone: 
a bicyclic fatty acid that acts by 
specifically activating chloride
channels on the apical aspect of 
GIT epithelial cells ==> producing a 
chloride-rich fluid secretion ==> 
softening of stool, increase motility, 
and promote spontaneous bowel 
movements.
