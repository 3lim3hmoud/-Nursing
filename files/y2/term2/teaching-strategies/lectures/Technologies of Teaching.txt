KEY DEFINITIONS
Technology
The systematic application of scientific or other organized 
knowledge to practical tasks.
Educational Technology
The use of technology to improve education — a systematic, 
interactive process for designing instruction or training used to 
improve performance. Also known as instructional or learning 
technology.
Core Purpose
Educational technology integrates technology into education to promote a more diverse learning environment and empower 
students to use technology alongside their regular assignments — improving efficiency, motivation, and access to learning.

E-LEARNING — DEFINITION & MODALITIES
E-Learning: Teaching using modern communication mechanisms — computers, networks, multimedia, and internet portals — to 
deliver information fast and cost-effectively, while enabling management, setup, measurement, and evaluation of learner 
performance.
FOUR MODALITIES OF E -LEARNING
Self-Paced Online
Individual learner accesses resources (databases, courses) 
via Intranet or Internet — e.g., solo online research.
Self-Paced Offline
Individual learner uses resources offline — hard drive, CD or 
DVD — without internet connection.
Group-Based Sync
Groups work together in real time via Intranet/Internet —
includes text chat, audio/videoconferencing.
Group-Based Async
Groups work over time with delayed exchanges — e-mail 
lists, threaded discussions, LMS forums.

IMPACT OF E-LEARNING ON TEACHING & LEARNING
Improves efficiency in both learning and teaching
Increases learner motivation and deepens understanding
Promotes collaborative learning across distances
Gives new approaches to learning and working
Provides new ways of interacting and social skill building
Paves the way for personality development
Helpful for self-evaluation — wide reach and consistent
User-convenient, flexible, and rich medium for students
Addresses different learning and cognitive styles
Self-pacing for slow and quick learners — reduces stress and 
increases satisfaction

E-LEARNING — ADVANTAGES & DISADVANTAGES
ADVANTAGES
Convenient
Self-service, on-demand, private, self-paced, flexible
Cost-Effective
Virtual environments, shared lessons, reduced material & travel costs
Consistent
Central content control — same quality for all learners
Media-Rich
Easier to understand, more engaging multimedia content
Repeatable
Access lessons as many times as needed
Easy Monitoring
Less admin work, more precise progress tracking
DISADVANTAGES
Unmotivated learners or those with poor study habits may fall 
behind
Lack of familiar structure and routine may take getting used to
Students may feel isolated or miss social interaction
The instructor may not always be available on demand
Slow or unreliable internet connections can be frustrating

VIRTUAL CLASSROOM — DEFINITION & FEATURES
A virtual classroom is an online teaching and learning environment where instructors teach remotely in real time using multimedia 
materials (slides, audio, video). It is the e-learning modality most similar to traditional classroom training — distinguished by its live, 
synchronous nature.
INSTRUCTOR CAPABILITIES
Moderate Participation
Control and manage student 
engagement and involvement
Display Materials
Share documents, slide decks, and 
multimedia files
Screen Sharing
Virtual whiteboard and real-time 
screen sharing features
Breakout Rooms
Divide participants into small groups 
for collaboration
Polls & Quizzes
Engage participants with interactive 
assessments
Record Sessions
Record, archive and manage session 
recordings

VIRTUAL CLASSROOM — BENEFITS & EXAMPLES
Distance Learning for Students
Enables working professionals, busy learners, and 
those with fewer financial resources to learn new 
skills.
Share & Monetize Training
Institutions can share and monetize courses without 
traveling — MOOCs, bootcamps, and online degree 
programs.
Grow Brand Presence
Universities can attract students beyond their 
geographic area by offering live virtual courses.
VIRTUAL CLASSROOM EXAMPLES
MOOCs
Massive Open Online Courses — Coursera, edX. 
Open enrollment, some accredited, free or fee-
based.
Bootcamps
Intensive focused training in tech, design, or data 
— Thinkful, General Assembly, Galvanize.
Online Degree Programs
Full university degrees delivered virtually —
recruiting online students beyond geographic 
limits.

MOBILE LEARNING (M-LEARNING) — OVERVIEW
M-Learning is a model of training that blends electronic and distance education with portable mobile devices such as 
smartphones and handheld computers — enabling anytime, anywhere learning.
KEY BENEFITS
Self-directed, individualized and self-paced learning
Interactive learning with engaging digital content
Promotes lifelong learning — education moves out of the classroom
Global delivery of learning materials
Complements and extends distance, online, and classroom learning
MOBILE DEVICE TYPES
PDAs / Pocket PCs
Palm-Pilots and handheld computers
Smart Phones
MP3, video and app-enabled mobile phones

M-LEARNING — ADVANTAGES & DISADVANTAGES
ADVANTAGES
Access lessons, video clips, and audio from anywhere — on buses, trains, 
public places
Interaction with fellow students and instructors is a great help
Portability — take your learning wherever you go
Psychological factor — device ownership increases motivation and 
commitment
Colorful, inviting material prompts repeated practice
More affordably priced; large percentage of population already owns 
devices
Flexible 24/7 access from any location
Each student learns at his or her own pace
Saves cost of learning materials and commuting expenses
DISADVANTAGES
Size Inconvenience
Small screens limit content display and usability
Limited Storage
PDAs have restricted storage capacities
Battery Life
Short battery life and frequent changes are a nuisance
Rapid Obsolescence
Devices may become outdated quickly
Limited Bandwidth
Wireless bandwidth may decrease with more users
No Printing Capability
Printing requires a network connection not always available
