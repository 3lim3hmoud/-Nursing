Records
Definition:
❑ Records the memory of the internal and external of transaction 
of an organization.
❑ Records contain a written evidence of the activities of an 
organization in the form of letters, circulars, reports, contract, 
invoice, vouchers, minutes of meeting, books of account, ….. etc.
❑ It is a written communication that permanently documents 
information relevant to a client’s health care management.
❑ It is a continuing account of the client’s health care needs..

3
Principles of Maintaining Records
❑ Specific purpose which should be clearly understood.
❑ Items on forms and in register should be conveniently grouped to 
make their completion as easy as possible.
❑ The wording should be easily understood, and where doubt is likely 
to arise, instructions to facilitates interpretation should be included.
❑ Records should permit some freedom of expression.
❑ Records which are required by the staff should be easily accessible to 
them.
❑ Provision for periodic review of all records to ensure that they keep 
pace with the changing needs of the program..

4
Principles of Maintaining Records
❑ Person responsible for maintaining records should be aware of their 
particular responsibility and every effort should be made to keep 
records up to date and accurate.
❑ Adequate supply of stationary to permit records to be maintained on 
the proper forms and in the proper register at all times.
❑ Sufficient number of filling cabinets and appropriate equipment to 
operate a filling system which is simple and safe and requires the 
minimum possible time.
❑ Adequate, safe, fireproof strong  arrangements..

Alphabetical Order
Alphabetical order is primarily used for organizing reference lists 
and filing patient records, not for the main body of a clinical note. 
The principles of good technical nursing writing emphasize clarity, 
accuracy, and standardized formats like SOAPIE or DAR..
Advantages of alphabetical order:
❖ Most people are familiar.
❖ Staff be able to learn and become comfortable with the system in 
a timely manner.
❖ Repetitive references can be avoided..

16
Numerically Order
❖ Numerically ordering records means arranging them in a sequence 
based on their numbers.
❖ Either in ascending order (lowest to highest) or descending 
order (highest to lowest). 
❖ This process, called sorting, makes data more accessible and readable. 
❖ Common systems for numeric ordering include
✓ Straight numeric filing (sequential order) and
✓ Terminal-digit filing (organizing by the last digits of a number).

17
Geographically Order
❖ Information is arranged alphabetically by geographically of place 
name.
❖ geographical ordering is a filing system that arranges records by 
location, grouping all documents related to a specific geographic 
area. 
❖ The specific method can range from simple alphabetical lists of 
places to complex, multi-layered hierarchies. 
❖ This method is especially useful for organizations with operations 
spread across multiple locations.

18
With Index Card Order
❖ An Index Card consists of heavy paper cut to a standard size, used 
for recording and storing small amount of discrete data.
❖ In addition, each organization should maintain:
✓ Cumulative records.
✓ Family records..

19
Guidelines for Documentation and Record Keeping 
Patient records should be:
❖ Based on fact, correct and consistent.
❖ Written as soon as possible after an event has happened.
❖ Written clearly and in such a way that the text cannot be erased.
❖ Written in such way that any alternations or additions are dates, 
timed and signed, so that the original entry is still clear.
❖ Accurately dated, timed and signed.
❖ Not include abbreviations, jargon meaningless phrase, irrelevant 
speculation and offensive subjective statements.
❖ Readable on any photocopies..
