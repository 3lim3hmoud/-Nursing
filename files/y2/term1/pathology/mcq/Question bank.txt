1. The black coloration in gangrene is primarily due to the formation of: 
a) Bilirubin 
b) Carbon monoxide 
c) Iron sulphide 
d) Deoxygenated hemoglobin 
Answer: c 
 
2. Which type of gangrene is characterized by occlusion of the arterial blood supply 
while veins remain patent? 
a) Moist Gangrene 
b) Gas Gangrene 
c) Dry Gangrene 
d) Infective Gangrene 
Answer: c 
 
3. A key pathological feature of Dry Gangrene is: 
a) Rapid onset due to venous and arterial occlusion 
b) Affects internal organs like the intestines 
c) Slow spread and the formation of a line of demarcation 
d) Excessive production of gases by bacteria 
Answer: c 
 
4. Which of the following is NOT a common cause of Dry Gangrene? 
a) Thrombosis 
b) Surgical Ligature 
c) Venous Obstruction 
d) Ergot poisoning 
Answer: c 
 
5. Moist Gangrene commonly occurs in which of the following situations? 
a) Senile gangrene of the lower limb 
b) When both arterial and venous supplies are occluded 
c) In organs with good collateral circulation

d) When only the venous supply is blocked 
Answer: b 
 
 
 
6. What is the initial event in the pathogenesis of Moist Gangrene in a strangulated 
hernia? 
a) Arterial occlusion 
b) Bacterial invasion 
c) Venous obstruction 
d) Putrefaction 
Answer: c 
7. Gas Gangrene is specifically caused by infection with: 
a) Streptococcus pyogenes 
b) Staphylococcus aureus 
c) Clostridium species 
d) Escherichia coli 
Answer: c 
 
8. What is the definition of edema? 
a) Massive tissue necrosis 
b) An abnormal accumulation of fluid in interstitial tissue and serous membranes 
c) Obstruction of blood vessels 
d) Inflammation of serous membranes 
Answer: b 
 
9. Which of the following is a cause of generalized edema? 
a) Localized venous obstruction 
b) Right-sided heart failure 
c) Filariasis 
d) A localized tumor 
Answer: b

10. Which of the following is a cause of localized edema due to lymphatic obstruction? 
a) Congestive heart failure 
b) Nephrotic syndrome 
c) Filariasis 
d) Liver cirrhosis 
Answer: c 
 
 
 
 
 
11. A patient presents with pitting edema in their ankle following surgery for breast 
cancer that involved lymph node removal. This is best classified as: 
a) Cardiac edema 
b) Inflammatory edema 
c) Lymphatic obstruction edema 
d) Renal edema 
Answer: c 
 
 
12. Which of the following is a direct cause of decreased plasma oncotic pressure 
leading to edema? 
a) High protein intake 
b) Liver disease 
c) Arterial hypertension 
d) Vasodilation 
Answer: b 
13. The "line of demarcation" in Dry Gangrene represents: 
a) The point of initial arterial occlusion 
b) The border between healthy tissue and gangrenous tissue 
c) The path of bacterial invasion 
d) The line where venous congestion begins 
Answer: b

1. Left ventricular hypertrophy most commonly occurs in which condition? 
A) Diabetes mellitus 
B) Hypertension 
C) Pulmonary embolism 
D) Hypothyroidism 
Answer: B 
 
2. Renal hypertrophy is commonly seen after: 
A) Urinary tract infection 
B) Contralateral nephrectomy 
C) Renal artery stenosis 
D) Diabetic nephropathy 
Answer: B 
 
3. Breast hyperplasia during puberty is classified as: 
A) Pathological hyperplasia 
B) Physiological hyperplasia 
C) Neoplastic growth 
D) Dysplasia 
Answer: B 
 
4. Endometrial hyperplasia is primarily caused by: 
A) Decreased progesterone 
B) Increased estrogen 
C) Increased testosterone 
D) Decreased luteinizing hormone 
Answer: B

5. Barrett’s esophagus is an example of which pathological process? 
A) Hyperplasia 
B) Metaplasia 
C) Dysplasia 
D) Neoplasia 
Answer: B 
 
6. Tumors classified as “locally malignant” are characterized by: 
A) Metastasis to distant organs 
B) Infiltration of surrounding tissues without metastasis 
C) Benign behavior 
D) Complete encapsulation 
Answer: B 
 
7. Which type of tumor arises from mesenchymal tissue? 
A) Carcinoma 
B) Sarcoma 
C) Adenoma 
D) Melanoma 
Answer: B 
 
8. The main difference between hypertrophy and hyperplasia is: 
A) Hypertrophy involves increased cell number, hyperplasia involves increased cell size 
B) Both involve increased cell size 
C) Hypertrophy involves increased cell size, hyperplasia involves increased cell number 
D) Neither involve change in cell size or number 
Answer: C

9. Papilloma is a benign tumor of which tissue? 
A) Glandular epithelium 
B) Protective surface epithelium 
C) Fatty tissue 
D) Smooth muscle 
Answer: B 
 
10. Adenoma is a benign tumor arising from: 
A) Protective epithelium 
B) Glandular epithelium 
C) Fat tissue 
D) Smooth muscle 
Answer: B 
 
11. Lipoma commonly occurs in: 
A) Uterus 
B) Subcutaneous fat 
C) Liver 
D) Brain 
Answer: B 
 
12. The microscopic picture of a lipoma typically shows: 
A) Spindle-shaped cells with cigar-shaped nuclei 
B) Vacuolated fat cells with flat nuclei compressed against the cell wall 
C) Branched fibrous tissue core covered by epithelial cells 
D) Malignant basaloid epithelial cells 
Answer: B

13. Leiomyoma is a benign tumor of: 
A) Smooth muscles 
B) Skeletal muscles 
C) Fatty tissue 
D) Glandular tissue 
Answer: A 
 
14. Carcinoma in situ is characterized by: 
A) Malignant cells invading the basement membrane 
B) Cytologic atypia without invasion of the basement membrane 
C) Benign epithelial proliferation 
D) Metastasis to distant organs 
Answer: B 
 
15. Most common sites for blood-borne metastasis include all EXCEPT: 
A) Bone 
B) Brain 
C) Liver 
D) Skin
