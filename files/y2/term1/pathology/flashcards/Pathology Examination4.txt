Part III: Essay & Short Answer Questions 
Q1. Factors Affecting Wound Healing 
Local Factors: 
• Type, size, and site of wound 
• Movement or lack of apposition 
• Infection (suppuration, gangrene, tetanus) 
• Local blood supply 
• Foreign materials (dirt, necrotic tissue) 
General Factors: 
• Age 
• General health (e.g. diabetes) 
• Drugs (e.g. steroids)

• Cardiovascular status 
• Dietary deficiencies (protein, Vitamin C, sulfur-containing amino acids) 
 
Q2. Complications of Repair 
Insufficient Fibrosis: 
• Ulceration 
• Sinus (blind-ending tract) 
• Fistula (abnormal communication between two epithelium-lined surfaces) 
Excessive Contraction: 
• Contractures (especially after burns) 
• Strictures (obstruction of tubes or channels) 
 
Q3. Epidermal (Implantation) Cyst 
Definition: 
A keratin-filled cyst caused by proliferation of epithelial cells trapped deep in a wound 
during regeneration.
